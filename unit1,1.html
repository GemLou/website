1.7 Describe legal guidelines and constraints
that impact digital projects.

One of the biggest legal guidlines that companies and idividuals must follow when working on certain digital projects is GDPR. GDPR, stands for General Data Protection Regulation. GDPR basically gives us our rights as users online, and this is so important in a world where our personal data is constantly being stored online. Data can be our name, photo's, IP address, bank details, email addresses, and much more.

GDPR allows us to ask for access to our personal information, and also ask the company we give our data to how they are using the data, we also have the right for our information to be deleted after a certain amount of time or if we ask to be withdrawn. There are many other ways that GDPR protects us, but how does this affect a digital project?

If you do not comply with GDPR rules, you could face fines and also a potential distrust between the customer and your company, as well as a possible scandal. So let's say you are making a wesbite, your website is selling makeup, first you need to figure out what data you will actually be storing and where this will be stored, because if someone asks where their data is and what it is being used for, you need to have it on hand. With most retail sites, your personal payment information as well as delivery information will be stored, so if you are making a shop site, you need to ensure your cutomers know you are taking their personal data. This can be implemented by using tick boxes, were the customer reads your disclaimer/terms of service and chooses to agree or disagree to these. With this being said, you also need to ensure that you have a set time when the data is destroyed after it has been put in the system, so you may have to create a database where the you can add a time tag for when the data needs to be destroyed.

Cookies are also something you will need to ask the user for consent for, cookies will be stored on the users computer and they will send information back to the site, based on the users web searches and how long the user spent on these sites, this means that cookies can be used as a way of marketing products through personalised ads based on the users searches and purchases, so you need to make the consumer very aware of this, how you use the cookies and if they want to opt-in for cookies.

Another thing I need to feel the need to talk about is copyright laws, this can really affect a digital project if you aren't following the laws clearly. This is actually something you need to remember even if you aren't selling something. If you are say writing a blog post about videogames, and you are using images as well as other sites information and other peoples writings to quote, you must must must, cite this. Add a link, give credit, quote and citate. Not doing this can be bad and you can plagiarise someone's work which can lead to fines and sometimes prosecution, as it is in fact a criminal offense, especially if you take something from a big site, or copy a logo; your project could be at risk.
